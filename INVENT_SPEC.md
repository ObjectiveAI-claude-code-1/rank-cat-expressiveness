Rank cat pictures by the expressive character visible in each image. Evaluate the cat's posture, expression, and body language for signs of personality and individuality. Look for specificity in what the cat is doing or appearing to feel — a piercing stare, a mid-yawn stretch, ears pinned back in annoyance, the rigid alertness of hunting focus, the boneless sprawl of deep sleep, pupils dilated in excitement, or a loaf position with eyes squeezed shut in bliss. Pictures where the cat's pose communicates something particular about its inner life should rank high. Pictures where the cat is sitting neutrally, facing away from the camera, or frozen in a generic pose that could belong to any cat anywhere should rank low. The key distinction is specificity versus generality: an image that reveals who this particular cat is in this moment ranks above an image that merely confirms a cat was present. When building sub-functions, focus on reading the cat's facial expression (eyes, ears, mouth, whiskers), body posture and tension, the specificity of the action or emotion being conveyed, and whether the pose feels particular to an individual animal or interchangeable with any other cat.
